# Method Scope

This note defines the boundary of the pseudorange-correlogram-inspired experiment in this repository. It is written to avoid overstating the result as an exact reproduction of the original paper or as a real UrbanNav/RINEX processing pipeline.

## What Is Reproduced

The project conceptually reproduces one high-level algorithmic idea from Vicenzo, Xu, Xu, and Hsu (2024): evaluating candidate receiver states by comparing candidate-satellite ranges with pseudorange observations and weighting the score by signal-quality-like information.

In this MiniLab, the searched candidate state is:

```text
[x, y, clock_bias_m]
```

where `clock_bias_m` is receiver clock bias expressed in meters. The experiment produces a candidate-score map and compares the toy candidate-search output with an OLS pseudorange solution under fixed synthetic clean and NLOS-dominated scenarios.

## What Is Simplified

- Positioning is performed in a 2D local coordinate frame, not full 3D ECEF/ENU.
- The candidate search uses a small local grid around a baseline estimate.
- The displayed XY score map is a slice through the best searched clock-bias value.
- Satellite/source positions are synthetic and simplified.
- Pseudorange measurements are synthetic.
- C/N0-like measurements are synthetic quality weights, not receiver C/N0 logs.
- LOS, multipath, and NLOS labels are generated by the project's toy measurement model.
- Multipath and NLOS effects are represented as synthetic positive pseudorange biases and increased uncertainty.

## What Is Not Reproduced

- The original paper's full UrbanNav/RINEX processing workflow.
- Real UrbanNav pseudorange ingestion.
- Real UrbanNav C/N0 ingestion.
- Real ephemeris-based satellite positions.
- IF-level direct position estimation.
- Carrier-phase processing.
- Skymask-based visibility processing.
- The original paper's exact figures, tables, parameter settings, or benchmark values.
- A real-time GNSS receiver or a validated urban navigation system.

## Data Provenance

- Public UrbanNav data used in this repository: a small UrbanNav-HK-Medium-Urban-1 TST ground-truth route subset, used as a route / ground-truth reference for the conceptual experiment.
- Public UrbanNav data not used yet: RINEX pseudorange observations, real C/N0 values, receiver logs, and skymask labels.
- Synthetic data generated inside this repository: satellite/source positions, pseudorange measurements, receiver clock bias, C/N0-like weights, LOS/multipath/NLOS labels, multipath bias, and NLOS positive bias.
- Paper-derived data not used: no values are manually copied from paper tables, and no values are digitized from paper figures.

## Why This Is a Conceptual Replication

This is a conceptual replication rather than an exact reproduction because it isolates the candidate-state scoring idea in a controlled toy environment. The experiment is useful for learning because the assumptions are visible, the pseudorange and weighting models are inspectable, and the result can be regenerated quickly. However, it does not validate performance on real UrbanNav measurements and should not be described as reproducing the original paper's experimental results.

## Next Step Toward a Closer Reproduction

The next academically meaningful step is to ingest real UrbanNav RINEX pseudorange and C/N0 measurements, connect them with ephemeris-based satellite geometry and visibility information, and compare the toy scoring method against real urban navigation logs. That would move this repository from synthetic conceptual replication toward a closer UrbanNav/RINEX-based study.
